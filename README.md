Canada Vapes – Customer Retention & Loyalty Signal Analysis
Project Overview
This project was completed as part of the Data Analytics Capstone in collaboration with Canada Vapes, an e-commerce retailer.
The company experiences strong monthly customer acquisition (500–700 new customers) but faces challenges in converting these customers into repeat buyers. The current number of returning customers is approximately 3,500, with a target to increase this to 5,000.
The objective of this project is to analyze early customer behavior—particularly after the first purchase—to identify key drivers of retention and develop data-driven strategies to improve customer loyalty.

Key Insights
•	Higher Average Order Value (AOV) is strongly associated with increased likelihood of customer retention
•	Customers who purchase multiple items in their first transaction show higher engagement
•	Revenue distribution is skewed, with a smaller number of high-value transactions contributing significantly
•	Customer demand is concentrated among a limited number of top-performing products
•	The first 7–14 days after purchase represent a critical window for retention efforts
Recommendations
•	Encourage higher first purchase value through product bundling strategies
•	Implement early post-purchase engagement (emails, offers, recommendations)
•	Promote high-performing products in onboarding and marketing campaigns
•	Introduce order value incentives such as free shipping thresholds
•	Target high-potential customers with personalized engagement strategies
